ü•§ Juice Ingredient Preparation Order

Step 1: Produce Washing

  • Wash all produce thoroughly under cold running water
  • Use vegetable brush for root vegetables (carrots, beets, ginger, turmeric)
  • Pat dry leafy greens and herbs

Step 2: Ingredient Prep

  • Remove kale and spinach stems
  • Core apples (keep skin on)
  • Peel citrus fruits, remove seeds
  • Peel root vegetables (beets, ginger, turmeric)
  • Pre-soak chia seeds over night in water for next shift (1 cup seed : 2 cup water)

Step 3: Juicing Order

  • Light colors first: Cucumber, apple, coconut water blends
  • Green juices: Kale, spinach, parsley combinations
  • Orange/yellow: Carrot, turmeric, orange combinations
  • Red juices last: Beet-based blends
  • Rinse juicer between major color changes

üçπ Cocktail Preparation Standards

  • Use proper glassware for each cocktail type (coupe, rocks, highball)
  • Measure all ingredients precisely using jiggers
  • Shake vigorously for 10-15 seconds when recipe calls for shaking
  • Double strain cocktails to remove ice chips and muddled ingredients
  • Express citrus oils over drink when garnishing with peels
  • Taste test complex cocktails before serving

ü•§ Juice Production Method

  • Measure ingredients according to recipe for individual order
  • Feed into juicer starting with softer fruits, ending with harder vegetables
  • Add ginger/turmeric last for maximum flavor extraction
  • Stir gently to combine if separation occurs
  • Use shaker and ice and pour into 16oz cup with fresh ice. Shaker and fine strainer will help dilute and chill the juice concentration for quality experience
  • Serve immediately to customer

üçπ Cocktail Service Standards

  • Present cocktail within 3-5 minutes of order
  • Garnish consistently according to recipe specifications
  • Serve at proper temperature (ice-cold for most cocktails)
  • Clean bar tools between each drink to prevent flavor contamination
  • Offer premium upgrades when appropriate

Quality Standards

Juice - Flavor Balance: Sweetness should come primarily from fruits. Adjust with green apple if too earthy/bitter. Ginger should warm, not overpower. Citrus should brighten, not dominate.

Cocktail - Balance: Proper spirit-to-mixer ratios. Citrus should brighten without overpowering. Sweetness balanced with acidity. Garnishes should complement, not mask flavors.

Juice - Texture & Appearance: Smooth consistency (fine strain used every time). Vibrant natural colors. Minimal separation (some is normal). No visible pulp or debris.

Cocktail - Presentation: Proper dilution through shaking/stirring. Clear cocktails should be crystal clear. Layered drinks should have distinct separation. Garnishes should be fresh and properly prepared.

Temperature & Service: Serve ice-cold immediately after preparation. All drinks made fresh to order. Store prepped ingredients properly to maintain freshness.
